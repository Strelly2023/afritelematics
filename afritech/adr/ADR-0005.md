## Summary

This ADR records a **constitutional bookkeeping correction** that restores
explicit consistency between finalized epoch history and registry seal
requirements after manifest‑driven identity attestation was formalized.

No constitutional law, epoch semantics, runtime enforcement behavior,
or authority boundaries were changed.

---

## Motivation

During audit and replay verification, a discrepancy was identified between:

- the constitutional rule that **epoch finalization requires registry reseal**, and
- historical epoch metadata that marked several epochs as `FINAL` without
  explicitly recording reseal completion.

Although the registry was correctly resealed in practice, the lack of explicit
metadata caused **audit‑layer ambiguity** and **replay reconstruction failure**.

This ADR documents the correction and its rationale.

---

## Non‑Goals

This ADR explicitly does **not**:

- Introduce new governance or execution powers
- Advance or modify epoch counters
- Change kernel, guard, runtime, or enforcement behavior
- Alter constitutional authority boundaries
- Modify cryptographic identities beyond documentation alignment

---

## Changes

The following **non‑temporal corrections** were applied:

- Explicitly marked epochs **1–3** as `resealed: true` and `seal_status: SEALED`
- Recomputed and confirmed the registry attestation hash using the canonical
  pre‑seal registry snapshot
- Restored documented consistency between declared seal state and sealed identity
