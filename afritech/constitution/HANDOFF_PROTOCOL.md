# AfriTech Constitutional Handoff Protocol

**Status:** OPERATIVE  
**Authority:** Procedural (Non-Constitutional)  
**Scope:** Constitutional Access & Activation Guidance  
**Purpose:** Preserve legitimacy across generational transition

---

## Purpose

This document defines the **procedural conditions under which constitutional action may be initiated** in AfriTech after completion of the foundational constitutional cycle.

It introduces **no doctrine**, creates **no authority**, and interprets **no constitutional text**.

It exists solely to prevent procedural ambiguity for future constitutional actors.

---

## 1. When Constitutional Action Is Legitimate

Constitutional drafting or amendment is legitimate **only** when at least one of the following conditions is met:

1. A demonstrated operational contradiction with settled constitutional doctrine.
2. Inability of existing governance structures to preserve constitutional telos.
3. Necessity to resolve one of the explicitly recognized future inscription obligations.
4. Systemic risk to constitutional continuity identified through doctrinal audit.

Constitutional action must **not** be initiated due to:

- intellectual curiosity
- perceived elegance
- theoretical completeness
- convenience or expediency
- interpretive preference

---

## 2. Required Preconditions Before Drafting

Before any constitutional drafting may occur, the following sequence is mandatory:

1. Conduct a doctrinal audit against settled constitutional sources.
2. Identify a concrete contradiction or necessity.
3. Explicitly declare constitutional necessity.
4. Define affected files and paths in advance.
5. Open a constitutional review window.
6. Proceed with drafting only after review completion.

Skipping any step invalidates constitutional legitimacy.

---

## 3. Constitutionally Authoritative Files

The following files are recognized as constitutional source artifacts:

```text
afritech/constitution/FOUNDATION_CLOSE.md
```

Any future constitutional source file must be:

- explicitly declared as constitutional
- placed under `afritech/constitution/`
- preserved without retroactive modification

No other files possess constitutional authority by default.

---

## 4. Explicit Prohibitions

Future constitutional actors must not:

- reinterpret settled doctrine through commentary
- modify constitutional source artifacts retroactively
- create parallel or explanatory constitutional documents
- draft outside explicit necessity thresholds
- rely on unstated founder context or intent

Any such action constitutes constitutional breach.

---

## Final Note

This protocol does not grant permission to act.

It defines **how legitimacy is preserved when action becomes unavoidable**.

Until constitutional necessity is explicitly established, restraint remains the correct posture.
