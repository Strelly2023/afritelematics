# 1. Settled Constitutional Facts

## I. Legitimacy Supersedes Enforcement

AfriTech has entered a constitutional phase in which enforcement alone is no longer sufficient for legitimacy.

A constitutional act may be mechanically valid and still constitutionally illegitimate.

From this point forward, constitutional evaluation occurs across two planes:

1. Procedural validity
2. Historical and identity faithfulness

Enforcement guarantees correctness.

Legitimacy determines constitutional continuity.

This principle is settled and irreversible.

---

## II. Judgment Is a First-Order Constitutional Reality

Judgment is constitutionally acknowledged as:

- real
- unavoidable
- non-computable
- requiring legibility

AfriTech rejects:

- total mechanization of constitutional interpretation
- hidden discretionary constitutional authority

Judgment must remain visible, bounded, preservable, and contestable.

Judgment is a constitutional act.

It is not a failure condition.

---

## III. Structured Disagreement Is a Constitutional Asset

Constitutional disagreement is recognized as a preservation mechanism.

AfriTech’s constitutional health is measured not by unanimity, but by its capacity to sustain legitimate disagreement without constitutional rupture.

Disagreement must be capable of:

- existing without delegitimization
- persisting without capture
- leaving historical trace

Suppression of disagreement for procedural convenience is constitutionally suspect.

# 2. Constitutional Telos

AfriTech exists:

**To preserve identity through principled reinterpretation across time, without dependence on authorship, and without surrender to convenience.**

# 3. Governing Evaluative Test

All future constitutional drafting, amendment, governance design, and institutional inscription shall be evaluated against the following governing question:

**Does this increase AfriTech’s capacity to endure legitimate disagreement across generations?**

If yes:

The act is constitutionally aligned.

If no:

The act weakens constitutional continuity regardless of technical elegance, procedural efficiency, or immediate utility.

This evaluative test is binding orientation for all future constitutional work.
